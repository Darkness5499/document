
### Common Sentence Structures in Daily English Communication

- **be about to + V**: sắp sửa làm gì
  *I’m about to leave.*

- **be supposed to + V**: lẽ ra / được yêu cầu phải làm
  *I’m supposed to finish this today.*

- **be meant to + V**: được thiết kế để / đáng lẽ để
  *This feature is meant to simplify the process.*

- **be used to + N / V-ing**: quen với
  *I’m used to working under pressure.*

- **get used to + N / V-ing**: dần quen với
  *You’ll get used to it.*

- **feel like + N / V-ing**: cảm thấy muốn
  *I don’t feel like going out.*

- **tend to + V**: có xu hướng
  *I tend to overthink things.*

- **be in the middle of + N / V-ing**: đang làm dở
  *I’m in the middle of a meeting.*

- **end up + V-ing**: cuối cùng thì (ngoài dự định)
  *We ended up working late.*

- **figure out + N / wh-word**: tìm ra, hiểu ra
  *We’re trying to figure out what went wrong.*

- **turn out (to be)**: hóa ra là
  *It turned out to be a configuration issue.*

- **be up to + N / V-ing**: đang làm gì / tùy bạn
  *It’s up to you.*

- **have a hard time + V-ing**: gặp khó khăn
  *I’m having a hard time focusing.*

- **make sense**: hợp lý
  *That doesn’t make sense.*

- **There’s no point in + V-ing**: không đáng
  *There’s no point in arguing.*

- **be likely to + V**
  *It’s likely to rain later.*

- **be willing to + V**
  *I’m willing to give it a try.*

- **be able to + V**
  *I won’t be able to join the meeting.*

- **be allowed to + V**
  *We’re not allowed to use our phones here.*

- **be responsible for + N / V-ing**
  *I’m responsible for maintaining this service.*

- **be in charge of + N / V-ing**
  *She’s in charge of onboarding new hires.*

- **be concerned about + N / V-ing**
  *I’m concerned about the timeline.*

- **be aware of + N**
  *Are you aware of this issue?*

- **be involved in + N / V-ing**
  *He’s involved in several projects.*

- **be satisfied with + N**
  *I’m not satisfied with the result.*

- **be focused on + N / V-ing**
  *Let’s stay focused on the main goal.*

- **be capable of + N / V-ing**
  *He’s capable of handling this task.*

- **be short on + N**
  *We’re short on time.*

- **be ahead of / behind schedule**
  *We’re slightly behind schedule.*

- **be on the same page (about)**
  *Let’s make sure we’re on the same page.*

- **be open to + N / V-ing**
  *I’m open to feedback.*

- **be ready for + N / to V**
  *Are you ready to move on?*

- **be under pressure**
  *The team is under a lot of pressure.*

- **be out of + N**
  *We’re out of options.*

- **be in favor of / opposed to + N**
  *I’m in favor of this approach.*
